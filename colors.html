About Our Color System

How the Flake System Works

Every color option above is a full broadcast flake system — not a partial or decorative scatter. Here's what that means for your floor.

🎨

Full Broadcast Coverage

Flakes are broadcast into the wet base coat at full coverage — meaning the entire floor surface is covered with no gaps. This creates uniform color and texture across the whole floor, edge to edge.

🔬

Embedded & Sealed In

After curing, excess flake is scraped away, and a UV-stable polyaspartic topcoat is applied over the top. The flakes are permanently embedded — they won't lift, peel, or chip out with normal use.

💎

We Bring Samples to Your Estimate

Color photos don't fully capture how flake blends look at scale. We bring physical sample boards to every free estimate so you can see the actual flake color and texture in your garage's light before deciding.

☀️

UV-Stable — Won't Fade or Yellow

Our polyaspartic topcoat is UV-stable, so the colors you see today are the colors you'll see in 10 years. This is a critical difference between our system and standard epoxy, which yellows under UV exposure.

🛡️

Slip-Resistant Texture

The flake broadcast creates a naturally textured surface that provides excellent traction — even when wet. This is one of the reasons full broadcast flake is the preferred system for garage and commercial floors.

🔧

Custom Blends Available

Don't see exactly what you're looking for in our standard blends? We can create custom color combinations using our flake inventory. Ask about custom options during your free on-site estimate.
