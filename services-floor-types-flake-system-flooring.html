Flake System Flooring Kansas City

Full Broadcast Flake Epoxy Flooring in Richmond, Kearney & the Kansas City region

The full broadcast flake system is our most popular residential and commercial floor option — and for good reason. It combines a highly durable epoxy and polyaspartic system with a decorative, textured finish in 16 signature color blends. The flake broadcast creates natural slip resistance, hides minor surface imperfections, and produces a floor that's as attractive as it is functional.

What "Full Broadcast" Means

Full broadcast means the flake chips are thrown into the wet base coat at 100% coverage — meaning the entire floor surface is covered with flake, edge to edge, with no bare spots. This is different from a "partial broadcast" or decorative scatter, which leaves significant bare areas between chips. Full broadcast provides superior slip resistance, better adhesion for the topcoat, and a more consistent finished appearance.

The System Stack

  1. Diamond grinding: Opens the concrete surface for mechanical bond
  2. Primer coat: Penetrates deep into the ground concrete surface
  3. 100% solids epoxy base coat: Applied to the primed surface
  4. Full broadcast flake: Color chips thrown at 100% coverage into the wet base coat
  5. Scrape and blow: Excess loose flake removed after curing; surface lightly abraded
  6. UV-stable polyaspartic topcoat: Final protective layer locks the flake in permanently

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions

How many color options do you offer?

+

We offer 16 signature color blends — 10 standard ¼" flake blends included in all standard installs, and 6 premium Hybrid blends available for a small upcharge. We bring sample boards to your free estimate.

Is the flake system slip-resistant?

+

Yes — the full broadcast flake creates a naturally textured surface with excellent traction, even when wet. This is one of the primary reasons flake systems are preferred for garage floors and commercial spaces.

Can the flake chips peel or come off over time?

+

No — the chips are permanently embedded between the base coat and the polyaspartic topcoat. Once cured, the flake is part of the coating system, not sitting on top of it. It cannot peel or separate under normal use.
