Epoxy Flooring Richmond, MO

Concrete Coatings Built for Richmond Homes, Garages & Shops

As HH Next-Level Epoxy's home base, Richmond is where our story started — and where we're still most active. Richmond homeowners and shop owners come to us because they've seen our work around town and want the same results for themselves. Whether it's a two-car garage off the square, a shop floor on a Ray County farm, or a commercial space downtown, we approach every Richmond project the same way: grind the concrete mechanically, repair what needs repairing, and build a floor system from the slab up.

Richmond garages often have older poured concrete — slabs from the 1970s and 80s that have seen decades of road salt, vehicles, and moisture. Many have hairline cracking, open control joints, or previous coatings that have long since failed. That's exactly what our prep process is designed to handle. We don't acid etch and hope it sticks. We mechanically grind every slab to create a permanent bond profile that industrial-grade epoxy and polyaspartic can penetrate and lock into.

Garage Floor Coatings in Richmond

Richmond garage floors take a beating year-round. Winter road salt tracked in from Highway 10 and Ray County roads, summer humidity, heavy lawn equipment, and everything stored in a working Midwest garage — our full flake broadcast system handles all of it. The decorative flake covers the concrete completely, creates natural slip resistance, and makes garage cleanup as easy as sweeping or mopping. Most Richmond single and two-car garages complete in a single day.

A Common Concern We Address in Richmond

One thing we see often in Richmond is garage slabs where previous owners applied a box-store epoxy that eventually peeled or bubbled. We can grind all of that off, prep the concrete correctly underneath, and install a proper system on top. A failed previous coating doesn't disqualify your floor — it just means prep takes a little longer.
