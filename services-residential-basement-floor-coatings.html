Basement Epoxy Flooring Kansas City

Basement Floor Coatings Built for Kansas City Moisture Conditions

Basements present a challenge garage floors do not: moisture vapor moving upward through the concrete slab. When this is not accounted for, the result is bubbling and delamination regardless of coating quality.

We test every basement slab for moisture vapor before recommending a system. When vapor levels are elevated, we specify a moisture-tolerant primer that creates a vapor barrier between the slab and the epoxy system.

Moisture Testing — The Step That Determines Everything

Basement floor coating failures almost always trace back to one skipped step: moisture testing. Concrete is porous and naturally wicks moisture from the soil beneath it. If you seal that concrete without testing first, moisture vapor pressure can build under the coating and cause delamination within months — regardless of how good the product is.

We test every basement slab before recommending a system. If moisture levels are elevated, we discuss the available options honestly: a moisture-blocking primer system, a breathable coating product, or in some cases, recommending that drainage or waterproofing issues be addressed before coating. We would rather delay a project than install a system that fails.

Basement Floor Options We Install

Depending on your basement's use, moisture situation, and aesthetic goals, we install several different basement floor coating systems:

  • Full flake epoxy and polyaspartic: The most popular option — same system as our garage floors. Durable, attractive, easy to clean, and available in 16 flake color blends.
  • Solid color epoxy: Clean, commercial-looking finish for utility spaces, storage areas, and finished basements where a single color is preferred.
  • Metallic epoxy: Decorative designer finish for finished basement spaces that serve as entertainment rooms, home gyms, or living areas.
  • Grind and seal: For basements where some breathability is important, a grind-and-seal system protects the concrete while allowing minimal moisture vapor movement.

Common Basement Floor Conditions We Work With

Kansas City area basements vary widely — here's what we commonly see and how we handle each situation:

  • Painted concrete that's peeling: We grind off the paint completely and prep the bare concrete
  • Efflorescence (white salt deposits): Indicates moisture migration — we address the source and prep appropriately
  • Hairline cracks and settlement: We fill cracks with structural epoxy filler before coating
  • Oil staining from previous use: Ground out and degreased during the prep phase
  • Previously tiled or vinyl-covered floors: Tile and adhesive are removed and the slab is ground to bare concrete

Whatever condition your basement floor is in when we arrive, we evaluate it thoroughly and recommend the right system for what we actually find — not what's easiest to install.
