School & Educational Facility Epoxy Floors in Richmond, Kearney & the Kansas City region

School floors are among the most demanding commercial environments we work in. They see hundreds of students and staff daily, get cleaned with institutional-grade disinfectants, take impact from dropped equipment and furniture, and need to perform consistently for years between maintenance cycles. We install epoxy systems specifically suited for educational environments throughout the Kansas City metro.

Why Schools Choose Epoxy Over VCT or Vinyl

VCT tile — the standard in most older school buildings — requires constant waxing and buffing to maintain its appearance. Vinyl sheet goods can delaminate and are difficult to repair. A properly installed epoxy and polyaspartic system eliminates both problems: no waxing, no buffing, seamless surface with no edges to lift, and a lifespan of 15+ years with basic maintenance.

  • Hallways and corridors: Full broadcast flake for slip resistance, visual appeal, and ability to hide minor scuffs and wear
  • Cafeterias: Chemical-resistant system that handles food spills and institutional cleaning products without degrading
  • Gymnasiums: Sport-appropriate coating systems with appropriate hardness and slip resistance for athletic use
  • Science labs and workshops: Enhanced chemical resistance for environments with solvent and acid exposure
  • Locker rooms: Moisture-tolerant, antimicrobial topcoat options for wet environments

Scheduling Around the School Calendar

We schedule school floor installations during summer breaks, winter breaks, and long holiday weekends when facilities are unoccupied. We can complete most school floor projects within a standard break period. We coordinate with facility managers on scheduling and access.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions

Do you work during school breaks?

+

Yes — we schedule school floor installations during summer break, winter break, and holiday periods to avoid disrupting school operations. We coordinate directly with facility managers on access and scheduling.

Is epoxy flooring safe for schools?

+

Yes — our systems are non-toxic once cured, meet slip-resistance requirements, and the seamless surface is more hygienic than tile because there are no grout lines where bacteria accumulate.

Can you do gymnasium floors?

+

Yes — we install sport-appropriate epoxy systems for gymnasium floors. We specify the correct system based on the athletic activities and surface requirements.

How long do school epoxy floors last?

+

With proper installation and basic maintenance — routine mopping with a pH-neutral cleaner — school epoxy floors typically last 15–20 years before any significant maintenance is required.
